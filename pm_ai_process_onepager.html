Where I pushed past the AI

The commission-bias catch

The model kept defaulting to Uber's playbook: commission-based pricing logic, even though NY runs zero-commission and that one fact changes what every lever actually does. It had no contextual grounding on the product or the geography, so it reached for the assumptions baked into its training instead of the brief in front of it. I caught this because I expected it going in; that kind of drift is what I watch for by default with any agent. The fix wasn't to argue it out of the assumption each time it surfaced. It was to change how I worked with it at the branching and diagnosing stages: instructed it to be collaborative rather than assertive there, surface the assumption and ask, instead of running confidently in the wrong direction.
