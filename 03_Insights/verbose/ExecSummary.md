# Summary for ops + exec

**Bottom line.** The congestion charge works in one narrow place and is getting credit for a lift it mostly did not cause; scaling it is the wrong move, and the real leak sits elsewhere.

**Do.** Keep the charge where it earns its keep, Auto Priority at peak held below its ~1.5x ceiling, and put the engineering behind the actual leak: ship the legible-deadhead offer card to cut driver cancellations, and start building supply-shaping, which buys completion with data we already own instead of rupees we don't have.

**Stop / hold.** Do not scale congestion pricing to the mass Auto tier or to 100%, and do not open Delhi or Kolkata on this evidence; the headline +14.9% is mostly a shrinking search base, demand that had already recovered before the charge shipped, and an off-peak version swap, while the charge itself does almost nothing for the mass tier and nothing at all for cancellation.

**One number to watch.** Completed rides per day, counted not ratioed, read Auto and Auto Priority apart; and before any city-wide ship, insist on a small randomized holdout, the one signal this entire dataset cannot stand in for.
