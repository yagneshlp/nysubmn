# Summary for ops + exec

**Bottom line.** The congestion charge works in one narrow place and is getting credit for a lift it mostly did not cause; scaling it is the wrong move, and the real leak sits elsewhere.

**Do.** Keep the charge where it earns its place: Auto Priority at peak, held below the ~1.5x ceiling. Put the engineering behind the actual leak: ship a legible deadhead compensation offer card to cut driver cancellations, and start building supply-shaping, which buys completion with data NY already owns instead of relying on capital it does not have.

**Stop / hold.** Do not scale congestion pricing to the mass Auto tier or to 100%, and do not scale based on this evidence. The headline +14.9% is mostly contributed by a shrinking search base, demand that had already recovered before the charge shipped, and an off-peak version swap. The charge itself does almost nothing for the mass tier and nothing at all for cancellation.

**One number to watch.** Completed rides per day, the count and not any ratio, read Auto and Auto Priority apart. Before any city-wide ship, insist on a small randomized holdout: the one signal this entire dataset cannot stand in for.
